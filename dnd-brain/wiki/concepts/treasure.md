# Treasure (2024)

Treasure encompasses everything of material value that adventurers can find, earn, or plunder — from loose coppers to legendary magic items.

## Coins

The five standard coin denominations, in ascending value:

| Coin              | Abbr. | Relative Value |
|-------------------|:-----:|----------------|
| Copper Piece      | CP    | 1/100 of a GP  |
| Silver Piece      | SP    | 1/10 of a GP   |
| Electrum Piece    | EP    | 1/2 of a GP    |
| Gold Piece        | GP    | 1 GP (baseline)|
| Platinum Piece    | PP    | 10 GP          |

Fifty coins of any denomination weigh 1 pound. Full exchange rates appear in the *Player's Handbook*.

## Trade Bars and Trade Goods

Merchants often deal in **trade bars** — metal ingots valued by weight — when moving large sums. A 2-pound silver bar is worth about 10 GP; a 5-pound gold bar is worth 250 GP. Similarly, **trade goods** (raw commodities) can substitute for coins: a pound of wheat trades for a copper piece, while a pound of saffron is worth 15 GP and a pound of platinum 500 GP.

## Gemstones

Gems are compact, lightweight, and easy to transport relative to their coin equivalent. They are organized by value tier, determined by rarity and quality:

| Tier     | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| 10 GP    | Azurite, lapis lazuli, turquoise, obsidian |
| 50 GP    | Bloodstone, moonstone, onyx, carnelian |
| 100 GP   | Amber, amethyst, jade, pearl, garnet |
| 500 GP   | Alexandrite, aquamarine, topaz, peridot |
| 1,000 GP | Blue sapphire, emerald, opal, star ruby |
| 5,000 GP | Black sapphire, diamond, jacinth, ruby |

The DMG provides random tables for each tier; a DM can roll once for a uniform collection or multiple times for variety.

## Art Objects

Art objects span a wide range: statues, jewelry, paintings, tapestries, ornate weapons and tableware. They are grouped by estimated value:

| Tier       | Examples |
|------------|----------|
| 25 GP      | Gold bracelet, silver ewer, ivory dice |
| 250 GP     | Bronze crown, bejeweled bracelet, carved ivory statuette |
| 750 GP     | Gold idol, obsidian statuette with gold inlay, painted war mask |
| 2,500 GP   | Platinum bracelet set with an emerald, gold music box |
| 7,500 GP   | Jeweled gold crown, jade game board with gold pieces, ceremonial gold armor |

## Treasure Themes

Monsters' treasure preferences are organized into four themes, which shape the mix of coins, gems, art, and magic items found in a hoard:

| Theme         | Typical Contents |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Arcana**    | Gemstones + magic items of an eldritch or esoteric nature |
| **Armaments** | Coins/trade bars + combat-focused magic items |
| **Implements**| Coins, trade bars, or goods + utility-focused magic items |
| **Relics**    | Art objects + magic items of religious origin or purpose |

## Random Treasure Hoards

The DMG provides a hoard table keyed to Challenge Rating (CR), giving approximate monetary rewards and a dice range for magic items found. As a benchmark, the DM should generate a hoard roughly once per session.

| Monster CR | Avg. Monetary Value | Approx. Magic Items |
|:----------:|--------------------:|:-------------------:|
| 0–4        | ~500 GP             | 0–3 (avg. ~1)       |
| 5–10       | ~4,400 GP           | 1–3                 |
| 11–16      | ~36,000 GP          | 1–4                 |
| 17+        | ~330,000 GP         | 1–6                 |

For monsters with strong treasure-hoarding tendencies (such as dragons), the DM can roll twice or double the total.

## Awarding Treasure

Key DM guidance from the 2024 DMG:

- **Quest rewards**: Use the same hoard table, substituting character level for CR.
- **Magic item pacing**: Use the magic item award guidelines (see [[concepts/magic-items]]) to ensure rarity distribution is appropriate for the party's tier of play.
- **Wish lists**: Encourage players to maintain a wish list of desired items; DMs can use this when deciding which items to place.
- **Overstocking**: An adventure can contain about 25% more items than the target count, anticipating that some will be missed.
