D&D has two rest types — Short Rest and Long Rest — that let adventurers recover resources between encounters.

## Short Rest

- **Duration:** At least 1 hour of light activity (eating, reading, tending wounds).
- **Benefit:** Spend any number of unspent Hit Dice to recover Hit Points. Roll each die, add your Constitution modifier, and regain that many HP.
- **Other benefits:** Some class features (e.g., Warlock spell slots, Fighter Second Wind) also recharge on a Short Rest.
- There is no hard limit on Short Rests per day, but a Short Rest must be an uninterrupted hour.

## Long Rest

- **Duration:** At least 8 hours; no more than 2 hours of light activity during that time.
- **Benefit:**
  - Regain all lost Hit Points.
  - Recover up to half your total number of Hit Dice (minimum of one die).
  - All expended spell slots are restored.
  - Features that recharge on a Long Rest reset.
- **Frequency limit:** A creature can benefit from no more than one Long Rest in any 24-hour period, and must have at least 1 HP to benefit.

## Hit Dice

- Every character has a number of Hit Dice equal to their character level; the die type depends on class (d6 for Sorcerer/Wizard up to d12 for Barbarian).
- Spending a Hit Die during a Short Rest: roll the die + CON modifier, regain that many HP (minimum 0).
- On a Long Rest, you recover up to half your total Hit Dice (rounded up).

### Hit Die by Class

| Die | Classes |
|:---:|---------|
| d6 | Sorcerer, Wizard |
| d8 | Bard, Cleric, Druid, Monk, Rogue, Warlock |
| d10 | Fighter, Paladin, Ranger |
| d12 | Barbarian |

## Exhaustion and Rest

The [[concepts/conditions#Exhaustion (Special Condition)|Exhaustion]] condition degrades over time if a creature skips food, water, or sleep. Each Long Rest removes 1 level of Exhaustion (provided the creature ate and drank enough). At Exhaustion level 6, the creature dies. Exhaustion is the only condition whose severity increases with repeated applications.
