# Magic Items (2024)

Magic items are extraordinary objects imbued with magical power. They turn up in monster hoards, hidden vaults, and forgotten ruins — and range from a simple healing potion to reality-altering artifacts.

## Rarities

Every magic item has a rarity that indicates its relative power and typical market value. Consumable items (except Spell Scrolls) are worth about half the listed value.

| Rarity      | Typical Value   | Availability              |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Common      | ~100 GP         | Many towns and cities     |
| Uncommon    | ~400 GP         | Cities                    |
| Rare        | ~4,000 GP       | Larger cities             |
| Very Rare   | ~40,000 GP      | Unusual vendors           |
| Legendary   | ~200,000 GP     | Extraordinary sources     |
| Artifact    | Priceless       | Unique; tied to the story |

Artifacts are unique, plot-relevant objects. They can only be destroyed through a specific, often quest-worthy method.

## Item Categories

Nine categories exist, each with its own activation and use rules:

| Category        | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| **Armor**       | Must be worn to function. |
| **Potions**     | Consumable; drinking takes a Bonus Action. |
| **Rings**       | Must be worn on a finger or equivalent digit. |
| **Rods**        | Scepter-like; can also serve as an Arcane Focus. |
| **Scrolls**     | Consumable; activating destroys the scroll. Any literate creature can attempt to use one. |
| **Staffs**      | Can double as a Quarterstaff and Arcane Focus. |
| **Wands**       | Can double as an Arcane Focus. |
| **Weapons**     | Magical versions of standard weapons; magic ammo counts as magical for rule purposes. |
| **Wondrous Items** | A catch-all category: bags, boots, cloaks, figurines, horns, and more. |

## Attunement

Some items require a creature to spend a short rest bonding with them before their full properties become available. Full attunement rules are in the *Player's Handbook*; key points:

- A creature can be attuned to **no more than 3 magic items** at a time.
- Attunement ends automatically if the creature dies, if a cursed item's curse is lifted, or if the item is removed from the creature for an extended period.
- Some items have **prerequisites** — a required class membership or the ability to cast at least one spell (not granted by another magic item).

## Charges and Recharge

Many items store magical energy in **charges**, which are spent to activate properties:

- The number of charges remaining is revealed when an *Identify* spell is cast on the item.
- A creature attuned to a charged item automatically knows its current charge count and how many it regains.
- Charges typically replenish at the **next dawn**; items on planes where dawn never occurs recharge at the DM's discretion.
- Rolling the maximum die result when an item regains charges sometimes means it regains no charges, then loses its magic permanently (item-dependent).

## Cursed Items

Curses are a hidden quality that standard identification — including the *Identify* spell — usually fails to detect. Characteristics:

- The curse reveals itself only when the character uses the item or when certain conditions are met.
- Once a character is attuned to a cursed item, they **cannot voluntarily end attunement** until the curse is removed (e.g., via *Remove Curse*).
- Effects vary widely: some curses inflict penalties or compulsions; others cause the item itself to demand compliance.

## Awarding Magic Items

The DMG provides guidelines for DMs distributing items across a campaign. A typical party earns roughly 100 magic items by level 20, spread across rarities:

| Tier (Levels) | Common | Uncommon | Rare | Very Rare | Legendary |
|:-------------:|:------:|:--------:|:----:|:---------:|:---------:|
| 1–4           | 6      | 4        | 1    | 0         | 0         |
| 5–10          | 10     | 17       | 6    | 1         | 0         |
| 11–16         | 3      | 7        | 11   | 7         | 2         |
| 17–20         | 0      | 0        | 5    | 11        | 9         |

Magic items are **not required** for characters to keep pace with the game — they are prizes, not necessities.

## Activating a Magic Item

Activating an item typically requires a **Magic action**, plus any special interaction described in the item's entry (e.g., speaking a command word, drinking, reading text). Spells cast through an item use the lowest possible spell level, require no components, and don't expend spell slots.
