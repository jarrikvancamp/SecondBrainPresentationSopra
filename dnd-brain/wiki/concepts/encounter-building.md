## Overview

The 2024 DMG replaced the older multiplier-based encounter-sizing method with a straightforward XP-budget system. Choose a difficulty, calculate a total XP pool for the party, then spend that pool on creatures using their stat-block XP values.

## Three Difficulty Tiers

| Tier     | What to expect |
|----------|---------------|
| **Low**     | Characters face a scary moment or two and likely spend some healing resources, but casualties are unlikely. |
| **Moderate**| Real danger: weaker characters can be taken out of the fight, and there is a slim chance of death. |
| **High**    | Potentially lethal; surviving requires smart tactics and possibly some luck. |

As a rough calibration, a single monster whose CR equals the average party level represents a Low encounter for four characters.

## XP Budget per Character

Cross-reference the party's level with the desired difficulty to find the per-character budget, then multiply by the number of party members.

**Representative values** (not an exhaustive table):

| Level | Low  | Moderate | High  |
|:-----:|-----:|--------:|------:|
| 1     |   50 |      75 |   100 |
| 3     |  150 |     225 |   400 |
| 5     |  500 |     750 | 1,100 |
| 10    | 1,600 |   2,300 | 3,100 |
| 15    | 3,300 |   5,400 | 7,800 |
| 20    | 6,400 |  13,200 |22,000 |

For all 20 levels, consult the full XP Budget per Character table in Chapter 4 of the DMG.

## Building the Encounter: Three Steps

1. **Choose difficulty** — Low, Moderate, or High.
2. **Calculate total budget** — per-character XP × number of characters.
3. **Spend the budget** — add creatures whose XP values (from their stat blocks) sum as close to the budget as possible without going over. Leftover XP is fine.

### Illustrative Example

A **Moderate** encounter for **4 level-3 characters**:
- Per-character budget = 225 XP → Total = 225 × 4 = **900 XP**
- You could build this with, for example, one CR 2 creature (~450 XP) plus several CR 1/4 or CR 1/2 minions to approach the budget.
- Or spend it on one CR 3 creature (~700 XP) and a handful of CR 1/8 guards to fill the remainder.

## Pacing and Resource Considerations

The 2024 system focuses on per-encounter difficulty rather than a strict daily encounter quota. The DM is responsible for the overall pacing — a session with back-to-back High encounters drains the party quickly; a single Low encounter won't tax resources much. Short rests allow characters to recover limited abilities between encounters, so spacing them matters even without a formal "encounters-per-day" budget.

## Pitfalls to Watch

- **High-CR creatures** — a single monster whose CR exceeds the party's level can eliminate a character in one action; use cautiously.
- **Too many creatures** — groups larger than two creatures per character amplify damage variance; include some fragile targets that drop quickly.
- **CR 0 creatures** — use sparingly or substitute swarm stat blocks to avoid clogging the action economy.
- **Complex stat blocks** — referencing more than two or three different stat blocks in one fight is hard to manage at the table.
