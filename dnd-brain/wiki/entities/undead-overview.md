# Undead Overview

Undead are creatures animated by dark magic or trapped spiritual energy — either reanimated corpses or spirits denied rest. They are defined less by a single stat block than by a shared set of traits and immunities that separates them from the living.

## What Unites Undead

- **Immunity to poison** damage and the Poisoned condition (they have no living metabolism to poison)
- **Immunity to exhaustion** (they do not tire)
- Most are immune to being **Charmed** or **Frightened**
- Many do not need to breathe, eat, drink, or sleep
- Frequently deal or are empowered by **necrotic** damage
- Often vulnerable or sensitive to radiant damage and sunlight

## The Undead Spectrum (low to high CR)

| Creature | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Skeleton | 1/4 | Animated bones reanimated by necromantic magic; resistant to piercing damage from arrows |
| Zombie | 1/4 | Shambling animated corpse; possesses Undead Fortitude, which can let it shrug off a killing blow |
| Ghoul | 1 | Flesh-devouring undead whose claws paralyze humanoid victims on a failed save |
| Specter | 1 | Incorporeal spirit that drains life force; cannot wear or carry physical objects |
| Wight | 3 | A dark-willed warrior who drains maximum hit points with its life drain and can raise the slain as zombie thralls |
| Ghost | 4 | Ethereal spirit tethered to a location or unfinished purpose; can possess humanoids |
| Wraith | 5 | Powerful incorporeal undead that drains max HP and spawns Specters from those it kills |
| Vampire Spawn | 5 | A humanoid transformed into a blood-drinking thrall bound to its vampire master |
| Vampire | 13 | Legendary apex vampire: regenerates, charms, climbs walls, and shapeshifts; weaknesses include sunlight, running water, and a wooden stake |
| Lich | 21 | An archmage who achieved undeath deliberately via a phylactery; if destroyed, it reconstitutes unless the phylactery is found and destroyed |

See [[entities/lich]] and [[entities/vampires]] for deeper coverage of those two apex undead.

## Encounter Notes

- Low-CR undead (Skeletons, Zombies) are most dangerous in large numbers; individually they are trivial.
- Mid-tier undead (Wights, Wraiths) punish parties who lack magical weapons or healing word access between fights because of their max-HP drain.
- High-CR undead (Vampires, Liches) are lair-based solo threats with legendary actions and regional effects.
