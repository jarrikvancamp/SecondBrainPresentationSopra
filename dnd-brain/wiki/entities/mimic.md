# Mimic

**Type:** Medium Monstrosity (Shapechanger)  
**CR:** 5  
**Alignment:** Neutral

## Overview

The mimic is a shapeshifting predator that disguises itself as inanimate dungeon furnishings—most famously a treasure chest—to ambush prey. It is one of the quintessential dungeon hazards of D&D.

## Signature Traits

- **Shapechanger:** At will, a mimic can reshape its body to resemble any Small or Medium object. The transformation is perfect enough to fool passive Perception, though a high enough Investigation check can spot subtle inconsistencies.
- **False Appearance:** While motionless in object form, the mimic is visually indistinguishable from a mundane item.
- **Adhesive (Object Form):** Any creature that touches or grapples the mimic while it appears as an object becomes adhered to its surface—grappled and unable to freely disengage.
- **Attacks:** Uses a pseudopod (bludgeoning) and a bite (piercing + acid damage). Both attacks benefit from the Adhesive grapple.

## Tactics

Mimics wait in areas where creatures are likely to interact with objects—dungeon corridors with chests, doors, or furniture. Once prey touches the disguised form, the mimic reveals itself and uses its grapple to prevent escape while dealing sustained damage.

## Notes

Mimics are believed to be related to gibbering mouthers and other aberrant ooze-adjacent creatures, though they are classified as monstrosities. They are entirely solitary and have no social structure.
