# Bag of Holding

*Wondrous Item — Uncommon — No Attunement Required*

See also: [[concepts/magic-items]]

## Overview

A Bag of Holding looks like a plain cloth sack roughly two feet across, but its interior exists in a pocket dimension — an extradimensional space far larger than the bag's physical opening.

## Capacity and Weight

- The interior holds up to **500 pounds** of material, with a total volume no greater than **64 cubic feet**.
- No matter how full it is, the bag itself weighs only **15 pounds**.
- Items retrieved from the bag require an action.

## Hazards and Risks

**Overfilling or destruction:** If the bag is filled beyond its limit, punctured, or torn while loaded, it ruptures. All contents are scattered into the Astral Plane, and the bag is destroyed.

**Air supply:** A living creature placed inside has only about 10 minutes of breathable air before suffocating. The bag is not a safe hiding place for anyone who needs to breathe.

**Interaction with other extradimensional spaces:** Placing a Bag of Holding inside another extradimensional container — such as a *Portable Hole* or a *Heward's Handy Haversack* — is catastrophic. The overlapping pocket dimensions tear open a rift to the Astral Plane. Both items are destroyed instantly, and every creature and object within 10 feet is pulled through the rift and deposited somewhere in the Astral Plane.

## Role in Play

The Bag of Holding is one of D&D's most iconic utility items. It solves the mundane problem of encumbrance, enabling characters to carry far more than their Strength would normally allow. The risk of interacting with other extradimensional items creates a memorable hazard that clever players and DMs alike can exploit.

It appears on multiple random magic item tables in the DMG (Uncommon tier, across Arcana and Implements themes).
