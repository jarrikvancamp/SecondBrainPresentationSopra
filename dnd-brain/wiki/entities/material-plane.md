The Material Plane is the central hub of the [[concepts/cosmology|D&D multiverse]] — where the elemental forces and philosophical currents of all other planes converge into the jumbled, thoroughly magical reality of mortal life.

## What It Is

The Material Plane is not a single world but an expanse containing countless distinct worlds (also called Prime Worlds), each situated within its own Wildspace system. Famous published settings — Faerûn, Greyhawk, Eberron, Dragonlance, and others — each occupy their own world on the Material Plane.

## Origin: The First World

Cosmological legend holds that all Material Plane worlds are shards of a primordial *First World* shattered long ago. Seeds from the great cosmic tree Yggdrasil scattered into the resulting void, taking root and growing into the individual worlds now spread across the plane.

## Relationship to Echo Planes

Two parallel dimensions share the same cosmological space as the Material Plane:
- **[[entities/feywild|Feywild]]** — a vivid, emotionally heightened reflection.
- **[[entities/shadowfell|Shadowfell]]** — a bleak, death-haunted mirror.

Passage between the Material Plane and these echoes can be accidental — travellers may step through a forest grove and find themselves in a wholly different reality.

## Transitive Connections

- The **[[entities/ethereal-plane|Ethereal Plane]]** overlaps the Material Plane as the Border Ethereal, enabling ghostly scouting and travel to the Inner Planes.
- The **[[entities/astral-plane|Astral Plane]]** connects the Material Plane to the Outer Planes; Wildspace systems are regions where these two planes overlap.

## Travel Between Worlds

Journeying from one world to another on the Material Plane requires extraordinary means: a spelljamming voyage through Wildspace and the Astral Plane, transit via the Feywild or Shadowfell, magical portals, or nexus points that exist simultaneously in multiple worlds.
