# Owlbears

**Type:** Large Monstrosity  
**Alignment:** Unaligned

## Overview

The owlbear is a classic D&D monstrosity—a large predator combining the body of a bear with the head, talons, and screech of a giant owl. It is a territorial and aggressive beast found in forests and dense wilderness.

## Variants (2024 Monster Manual)

| Variant | Approx. CR | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Owlbear | 3 | Standard predator |
| Primeval Owlbear | ~6–9 | Larger, more powerful ancient variant |

## Signature Traits

- **Multiattack:** Strikes with both a beak attack and a claw attack in one turn.
- **Rend (Owlbear):** When both attacks hit the same target, the owlbear deals additional damage as it tears into flesh—reinforces the importance of hit-or-miss probability.
- **Screech (Primeval):** The primeval variant can unleash a terrifying shriek that frightens nearby creatures.
- **Keen Sight and Smell:** Advantage on Perception checks relying on sight or smell makes it very hard to ambush.

## Ecology & Behavior

Owlbears are purely instinct-driven predators with no language or social structure beyond mated pairs and offspring. They defend nesting territory ferociously. Legends vary on whether they were magically created by a wizard's experiment or simply a natural species—neither position is authoritative.

The Primeval Owlbear is a significantly more dangerous variant, likely representing an ancient or oversized specimen of the species, and presents a meaningful step up in threat level.
