# Hags

Hags are ancient, wicked crones that blend Fey and monstrous origins. They are cunning spellcasters who prize bargains, manipulation, and cruelty above all else. Most hags can alter their appearance to look like old women — or sometimes beautiful ones — hiding their true, hideous nature. They trade in favors, secrets, and souls.

## Hag Types

### Sea Hag (CR 2)
The most visceral and overtly horrifying variety. Sea Hags lurk in sunless coastal waters and caves. Their ugliness is weaponized — a sustained gaze from a Sea Hag can terrify even brave warriors into catatonia, and their most powerful look can stop a heart outright.

### Green Hag (CR 3)
A woodland deceiver who can project illusory beauty to lure victims. Green Hags enjoy making bargains they know will go wrong for the other party. They mimic voices, curse enemies, and often covet something the target cannot easily give.

### Night Hag (CR 5)
The most planar and predatory of the three. Night Hags slip into the Ethereal Plane to invade the dreams of sleeping mortals, draining their life force night after night. They harvest souls and trade them on the lower planes. Night Hags carry a heartstone used to shift between planes.

### Arch-hag (High CR)
The pinnacle of hag power — a creature of great age and accumulated cruelty, wielding magic far beyond what ordinary hags command.

## Covens

When exactly three hags gather and agree to form a coven, they collectively gain access to a shared pool of powerful spellcasting far beyond any individual's capability. The coven acts as a gestalt magical entity; losing even one member dissolves the bond and strips those bonus abilities. Covens deliberately include hags of different types for complementary strengths. This makes a trio far more dangerous than the sum of three separate hags.

## Encounter Notes

- Hags rarely fight fair; expect illusions, darkness, and prepositioned allies.
- Any bargain with a hag should be evaluated for hidden consequences — they love technicalities.
- Hag lairs often include charmed creatures acting as unwitting guards.
