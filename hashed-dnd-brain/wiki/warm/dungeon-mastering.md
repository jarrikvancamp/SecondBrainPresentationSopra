## Running the Game

### The Play Loop
Three-step repeating cycle: **Describe** → **Players Act** → **Resolve & Narrate**. Descriptions ~3 sentences before inviting questions. Outside combat, multiple characters act simultaneously.

### When to Roll
Call for a d20 Test only when **both** success and failure are possible and failure has meaningful consequences. Skip rolls for trivial or impossible tasks.

| Roll Type | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Ability check | Character actively attempts something |
| Saving throw | Character reactively resists/dodges |
| Attack roll | Character tries to hit a target |

**Group checks:** succeed if ≥ half participants pass; avoid when a single failure dooms everyone.

**Advantage/Disadvantage:** roll 2d20, keep higher/lower. Multiple sources don't stack; one of each cancels.

**Success at a cost:** on a near miss (fail by 1–2), optionally allow success with a complication. Fail by 5+ can trigger a worse outcome.

### DC Scale

| Difficulty | DC | Practical Note |
|---|:---:|---|
| Very Easy | 5 | Most characters succeed; skip unless unusual |
| Easy | 10 | ~50% chance with no ability/proficiency bonus |
| Moderate | 15 | Needs a decent score or proficiency |
| Hard | 20 | Needs both solid score and proficiency |
| Very Hard | 25 | Out of reach for low-level characters |
| Nearly Impossible | 30 | Near-perfect conditions required even at level 20 |

DM-set save DCs stay **10–20**. Creature-driven formula: **DC = 8 + ability modifier + Proficiency Bonus**.

## Encounter Building

### Difficulty Tiers

| Tier | Effect on Party |
|---|---|
| **Low** | Scary moment or two; some healing resources spent; casualties unlikely |
| **Moderate** | Real danger; weaker characters can drop; slim death chance |
| **High** | Potentially lethal; requires smart tactics and luck |

Calibration: a single creature whose CR = average party level ≈ Low encounter for 4 characters.

### XP Budget per Character

Multiply by party size for total encounter budget.

| Level | Low | Moderate | High |
|:---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 50 | 75 | 100 |
| 3 | 150 | 225 | 400 |
| 5 | 500 | 750 | 1,100 |
| 10 | 1,600 | 2,300 | 3,100 |
| 15 | 3,300 | 5,400 | 7,800 |
| 20 | 6,400 | 13,200 | 22,000 |

Full table: DMG Chapter 4.

### Build Steps
1. Choose difficulty (Low/Moderate/High).
2. Total budget = per-character XP × party size.
3. Spend on creatures (stat-block XP values); leftover XP is fine.

### Pacing & Pitfalls
- No formal encounters-per-day quota; DM controls pacing. Short rests between encounters matter for resource recovery.
- **High-CR solo monsters** can one-shot characters — use cautiously.
- **Mob-heavy fights** (>2 creatures/character) amplify damage variance; include fragile targets.
- **CR 0 creatures** clog action economy; use swarm stat blocks instead.
- Limit distinct stat blocks per fight to 2–3 for manageability.

## DM's Toolbox

### Chases
Standard movement makes chases deterministic; chase rules add randomness via Initiative order.
- Each participant can Dash; after **3 + CON modifier** Dashes, must make **DC 10 CON save** each turn or gain 1 Exhaustion (5 = drop out).
- End of each round (Init 0): quarry makes **Dexterity (Stealth)** check vs. lead pursuer's Passive Perception to escape. Crowded terrain → Advantage; open terrain → Disadvantage.
- Each turn: roll 1d12 and check Urban or Wilderness Chase Complications table for obstacles affecting the next creature in order.

### Environmental Hazards
Extreme cold/heat, frigid water, high altitude, heavy precipitation, slippery ice, strong wind, wild/dead magic zones. Each imposes penalties (reduced Speed, conditions, or mandatory saves).

### Static Hazards
Rated **nuisance** or **deadly** per level band. Exercise caution introducing a hazard to characters one band below its rating.

| Hazard | Effect |
|---|---|
| Green Slime | Corrodes nonmagical metal/wood; ongoing acid damage |
| Razorvine | Slashing damage on contact/movement |
| Brown/Yellow Mold | Cold damage or spore effects nearby |
| Poisonous Gas | CON save vs. damage/conditions |
| Quicksand/Webs/Rockslide/River Styx | Varied mechanical effects |

### Traps
Defined by **trigger**, **severity**, and **duration** (instantaneous / timed / until destroyed).

Common triggers: pressure plate, trip wire, doorknob, wrong key, proximity.

| Levels | Nuisance Damage | Deadly Damage | Attack Bonus | Save DC |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 1–4 | ~1d10 | ~2d10 | +4 | 10–12 |
| 5–10 | ~2d10 | ~4d10 | +4/+8 | ~14 |
| 11–16 | ~4d10 | ~10d10 | +8 | ~17 |
| 17–20 | ~10d10 | ~18d10 | +8 | 19–21 |

Use traps sparingly. Best traps are quick bypasses or teamwork puzzles.

### Poisons

| Type | Delivery |
|---|---|
| Contact | Touch with bare skin |
| Ingested | Swallowed as full dose |
| Inhaled | Powder/gas in 5-ft cube; holding breath doesn't help |
| Injury | Applied to weapon; activates on piercing/slashing damage |

Harvesting from creature: **1d6 minutes**, **DC 20 Intelligence (Nature) + Poisoner's Kit**. Fail by 5+ = self-exposure.

### Random Tables
Chapter 3 DMG provides: chase complications, monster personality, NPC attitudes, settlement traits. Use for improvisation.

## Creating Adventures

An adventure is a self-contained story built from combat, exploration, and social encounters. Players' choices shape how it unfolds.

### Four-Step Process
1. **Lay Out the Premise** — core conflict, setting, and what makes it interesting.
2. **Draw In the Players** — hooks tied to character goals.
3. **Plan Encounters** — scenes from start to finish.
4. **Bring It to an End** — likely endings and rewards.

### Conflict Scope by Tier

| Levels | Scale |
|---|---|
| 1–4 | Local (village, dungeon) |
| 5–10 | Regional (realm, kingdom) |
| 11–16 | National or world-shaking |
| 17–20 | Planar or cosmic |

### Hooks

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Patron | NPC commission, job board, or letter |
| Supernatural | Divine vision, prophecy, or omen |
| Happenstance | Discovered letter, disrupted festival, chance encounter |

### Encounter Design
Each encounter has an **objective** and an **obstacle**. Functions: advance goal, frustrate progress, or reveal information.

- **Combat:** vary elevation, terrain hazards, mixed enemy types.
- **Exploration:** traps, secret doors, environmental dangers.
- **Social:** negotiation, persuasion, deception.

Sample objectives: protect NPC, retrieve object, stop ritual, sneak past guards, make peace between factions, eliminate single target.

### Pacing
- Alternate encounter types; reserve hardest for the climax.
- Rests vs. urgency: scatter safe-rest windows deliberately; interrupt occasionally to maintain stakes.
- Random encounters drain resources, create urgency, or provide story moments.

### Structure
- **Linear:** scenes in sequence — clear but risks railroading. Plan multiple ways key events can occur.
- **Open/point-crawl:** multiple simultaneous threads; unresolved threads escalate.

### Endings & Rewards
Climax should be telegraphed by foreshadowing. **Denouement:** distribute treasure, roleplay aftermath, seed next adventure.

Climax shapes: final boss, pursuit, race against time, simultaneous rites, betrayal, escaping villain.

Rewards: treasure (individual loot + hoards by CR), quest rewards, reputation, titles, contacts. See also [[magic-items]].

## Creating Campaigns

A campaign links two or more adventures into a larger story — like a TV series where each episode feeds a bigger arc.

### Four-Step Process
1. **Lay Out the Premise** — core conflicts and tone-fitting setting.
2. **Draw In the Players** — memorable opening; understand each character's goals.
3. **Plan Adventures** — smaller conflicts feeding the main one; flesh out antagonists and locations.
4. **Bring It to an End** — target level and resolution of major conflicts.

### Campaign Journal
Track sessions, NPCs, locations, unresolved threads. Enables foreshadowing (plant details early, pay off later). Keep an "adventure stockpile" for surprises.

### Character Arcs
- **Goals** — short-term reasons to adventure.
- **Ambitions** — long-term personal aspirations.
- **Quirks/whims** — emerge in play.

Weave character-focused adventures in occasionally. Give meaningful victories ~every 5 levels.

### Conflicts and Tiers of Play

| Tier | Levels | Scope |
|---|:---:|---|
| Local Heroes | 1–4 | Village, dungeon, local threat |
| Heroes of the Realm | 5–10 | Region, kingdom |
| Masters of the Realm | 11–16 | Nations, world-shaking events |
| Masters of the World | 17–20 | Planar or cosmic stakes |

Maintain ~3 active conflicts at different scales. When one resolves, replace with a broader one — ideally revealing the old conflict was part of the new.

### Campaign Settings & Flavors
Published settings (lore done; players may know it) vs. homebrew (full control). Define: factions, magic prevalence, key mysteries, role of gods. See also [[cosmology]].

Flavors: heroic, sword and sorcery, epic (good vs. evil), mythic, horror, war, science-fantasy. Can mix.

### Session Zero
Pre-campaign alignment session.

| Topic | Key Decisions |
|---|---|
| Character creation | Vary classes; set start level (Lv 1 = new groups, Lv 3 = experienced) |
| Party cohesion | Shared history, bonding event, mutual patron, or happenstance |
| Setting the stage | Starting location, world events, house rules |

Start with a single detailed hub + 1–2 nearby adventure locations. Expand outward as the campaign demands.

### Episodes vs. Serials
- **Episodic:** stand-alone adventures; good for infrequent groups.
- **Serialized:** outcomes feed forward; rewards consistent attendance and builds momentum.

Link serials: end one adventure with an object, fleeing villain, or clue leading into the next.

### Player Investment
Recurring anchors: community the party cares about, home base ([[bastions]]), trusted NPC ally, villain with a sympathetic quality. Incorporate player-provided details (smells, trinkets, memories).

**Break episodes** (low-stakes, off-arc) every 3–5 levels to breathe and develop relationships.

### Ending a Campaign
Wrap major conflicts and character personal goals. Natural conclusion beats forced level 20. If enthusiasm wanes: solicit new ideas, rotate DM, or plan a grand finale. Past campaign events become the legends of the next one.

## Bastions

Optional system (DM opts in; individual players may opt in/out). A personal stronghold — home, refuge, power base.

### Gaining a Bastion
Unlocked at **level 5**. Form chosen by player (tower, shrine, keep, guildhall, etc.). Multiple characters can merge Bastions into one structure with separate facility rosters.

### Bastion Turns
One turn = **7 in-game days**. Turns happen whether or not the character is present. Default order when absent and out of contact: **Maintain**. DM may slow cadence (e.g., one turn/month) for long downtime periods.

### Basic Facilities
Every Bastion starts with **2 free basic facilities** (one Cramped, one Roomy). Options: Bedroom, Dining Room, Parlor, Courtyard, Kitchen, Storage. No mechanical effects; enrich roleplay.

| Space | Add Cost | Add Time | Enlarge Cost | Enlarge Time |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Cramped | 500 GP | 20 days | — | — |
| Roomy | 1,000 GP | 45 days | 500 GP | 25 days |
| Vast | 3,000 GP | 125 days | 2,000 GP | 80 days |

### Special Facilities
Gained through **level advancement** (not purchased). Provide crafting, research, recruiting, and other mechanical benefits. May swap one per level-up.

| Level | Total Special Facilities |
|:---:|:---:|
| 5 | 2 |
| 9 | 4 |
| 13 | 5 |
| 17 | 6 |

| Facility | Lvl | Order | Facility | Lvl | Order |
|---|:---:|---|---|:---:|---|
| Arcane Study | 5 | Craft | Library | 5 | Research |
| Armory | 5 | Trade | Sanctuary | 5 | Craft |
| Barrack | 5 | Recruit | Smithy | 5 | Craft |
| Garden | 5 | Harvest | Storehouse | 5 | Trade |
| Workshop | 5 | Craft | Gaming Hall | 9 | Trade |
| Laboratory | 9 | Craft | Training Area | 9 | Empower |
| Teleportation Circle | 9 | Recruit | Archive | 13 | Research |
| Meditation Chamber | 13 | Empower | Menagerie | 13 | Recruit |
| Demiplane | 17 | Empower | Guildhall | 17 | Recruit |
| War Room | 17 | Recruit | | | |

### Bastion Orders

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Craft** | Hirelings produce a supported item |
| **Empower** | Facility grants a temporary benefit to a creature |
| **Harvest** | Hirelings gather a produced resource |
| **Maintain** | Upkeep only; no other orders; triggers Bastion Event roll |
| **Recruit** | Hirelings bring new creatures (including Defenders) |
| **Research** | Hirelings gather information |
| **Trade** | Hirelings buy/sell stored or produced goods |

Each facility comes with hirelings whose wages are covered by facility income.

### Defenders & Fortifications
Defenders recruited via the Recruit order; first resource lost in an attack. Defensive walls: **250 GP per 5-ft. square, 10 days/square**; fully enclosed Bastion reduces Defender losses.

### Bastion Events (d100, on Maintain order)

| Roll | Event |
|:---:|---|
| 01–50 | All Is Well |
| 51–55 | Attack (Defenders lost; facility may shut down) |
| 56–58 | Criminal Hireling (bribe or lose hireling) |
| 59–63 | Extraordinary Opportunity (500 GP → recognition + second roll) |
| 64–72 | Friendly Visitors (pay for facility use; earn 1d6 × 100 GP) |
| 73–76 | Guest (dignitary, refugee, mercenary, or friendly monster) |
| 77–79 | Lost Hirelings (one facility offline one turn) |
| 80–83 | Magical Discovery (free Uncommon potion or scroll) |
| 84–91 | Refugees (2d4 folk; earn 1d6 × 100 GP) |
| 92–98 | Request for Aid (send Defenders; risk loss but earn reward) |
| 99–00 | Treasure (art object or [[magic-items\|magic item]]) |

Events can be played as NPC-focused cutscenes between turns.

### Fall of a Bastion
Lost via **divestiture** (voluntary), **neglect** (no orders for turns = character level), or **ruination** (e.g., Deck of Many Things Ruin card). After a fall, character may establish a new Bastion with the same facility count their level permits.
