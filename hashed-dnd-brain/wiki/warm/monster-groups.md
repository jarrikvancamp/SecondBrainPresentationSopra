# Monster Groups

Six major monster families for 5e (2024). See also [[cosmology]], [[iconic-monsters]].

---

## Demons

Chaotic Evil fiends native to the Abyss — an infinitely layered plane of malevolence. No hierarchy beyond raw power; they destroy for the pleasure of it. Immune to fire, poison, and lightning; stronger specimens resist cold and non-magical physical damage. Summoned demons must be magically bound or they turn on their summoner. Demon lords (CR 20+) rule individual Abyssal layers. Internal rivalries can be exploited by clever parties.

| Demon | CR | Role / Distinguishing Trait |
|---|---|---|
| Dretch | 1/4 | Weakest form; nauseating gas cloud |
| Shadow Demon | 4 | Incorporeal; vulnerable to radiant and sunlight |
| Chasme | 6 | Fly-like; droning hum renders creatures unconscious |
| Hezrou | 8 | Toad brute; stench incapacitates nearby foes |
| Glabrezu | 9 | Four-armed tempter (two pincers, two humanoid arms); offers dark bargains |
| Vrock | 9 | Vulture-headed; opens with spore cloud and piercing screech |
| Nalfeshnee | 13 | Porcine-winged abyssal judge; bolts of unholy light |
| Marilith | 16 | Six-armed serpentine general; wields six weapons, leads armies |
| Balor | 19 | Apex lord-tier; fire-wreathed, flaming sword + whip, death-burst explosion on death |

### Blood War
The eternal grinding conflict between demons and devils for supremacy over the Lower Planes. Demons fight with overwhelming numbers and chaos; devils counter with discipline and strategy. Neither side has gained a decisive edge in millennia. Adventurers near the front face danger from both factions.

## Devils

Lawful Evil fiends from the Nine Hells of Baator. The opposite of demons: highly organized, hierarchical, and strategic. They pursue domination through contract, manipulation, and military order. All devils share immunity to fire and poison, resistance to cold and non-magical physical damage, and darkvision.

**Hierarchy:** Rigid rank-and-file with lesser devils serving greater under pain of destruction. Archdevils rule each of the Nine Hells' layers; Asmodeus sits at the apex. Greater devils scheme constantly to ascend.

| Devil | CR | Role / Distinguishing Trait |
|---|---|---|
| Imp | 1 | Tiny shapeshifting spy; common evil-spellcaster familiar |
| Spined Devil | 2 | Small flying scout; pelts enemies with burning spines |
| Bearded Devil | 3 | Infantry soldier; glaive inflicts festering wounds resisting healing |
| Barbed Devil | 5 | Spine-covered overseer; damages anything that strikes or grapples it |
| Chain Devil | 8 | Animates chains to wrap and torment foes slowly |
| Bone Devil | 9 | Insectile commander; leads lesser devils, venomous tail sting |
| Horned Devil | 11 | Powerful flying warrior; massive fork and armored claws |
| Erinyes | 12 | Corrupted-angel warriors; bow + binding rope |
| Ice Devil | 14 | Arthropod-shaped commander; enforces coldest Hells with cold magic |
| Pit Fiend | 20 | Supreme infernal general; commands legions, devastating magic, legendary actions |

*See Blood War under Demons above. Devils view the war as strategically advantageous — it keeps demons away from the mortal world.*

## Giants

Huge-or-larger humanoid-shaped beings of tremendous physical power (Giant creature type). Governed culturally and spiritually by the **Ordning** — a divinely ordained rank structure determining social standing within and across giant kinds.

**The Ordning:** Hill Giants at the bottom, Storm Giants at the apex. Rank within a kind is measured by prowess, wealth, or wisdom depending on subtype. Inter-kind interactions are shaped by Ordning position. Most giants have culture, language, and social ambitions — not mindless brutes.

| Giant | CR | Ordning Rank / Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Hill Giant | 5 | Lowest; gluttonous and dull-witted, driven by appetite and brute force |
| Stone Giant | 7 | Reclusive cave-dwellers; philosophical, exceptional boulder-throwers |
| Frost Giant | 8 | Warlike tundra raiders; prize battle trophies and displays of strength |
| Fire Giant | 9 | Disciplined smiths and slavers; military structure, volcanic strongholds |
| Cloud Giant | 9 | Aristocratic sky-castle dwellers; range from arrogant to benevolent |
| Storm Giant | 13 | Highest; wisest and most powerful; prophetic, isolated, closest to divine purpose |

Fire and Frost Giants are most frequently antagonistic due to aggressive expansionism. Cloud and Storm Giants may serve as reluctant allies or neutral powers.

## Hags

Ancient, wicked crones of Fey and monstrous origin. Cunning spellcasters who prize bargains, manipulation, and cruelty. Can alter appearance to seem like old (or beautiful) women, hiding their true hideous nature. They trade in favors, secrets, and souls. Never fight fair — expect illusions, darkness, and charmed creature guards. Any bargain should be scrutinized for hidden technicalities.

| Hag | CR | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Sea Hag | 2 | Coastal lurker; weaponized ugliness — sustained gaze causes catatonic terror, strongest look can stop a heart |
| Green Hag | 3 | Woodland deceiver; projects illusory beauty, mimics voices, curses enemies, crafts ruinous bargains |
| Night Hag | 5 | Planar predator; invades dreams via Ethereal Plane to drain life force night after night; harvests and trades souls; carries a heartstone for planar shifting |
| Arch-hag | High | Pinnacle hag; great age and accumulated cruelty, magic far beyond ordinary hags |

### Covens
Exactly three hags who agree to form a coven become a gestalt magical entity with a shared pool of powerful spellcasting beyond any individual's reach. Losing even one member dissolves the bond and strips all bonus abilities. Covens deliberately mix hag types for complementary strengths — a trio is vastly more dangerous than three separate hags.

## Goblinoids

A family of related **Fey** creatures — Goblins, Hobgoblins, and Bugbears — sharing common ancestry. In 5e (2024) all three are classified as Fey (not Humanoid), reflecting Feywild origins. Dramatically different in temperament, tactics, and social structure.

| Member | CR Range | Size | Tactics | Key Trait |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Goblin (Minion/Warrior/Boss/Hexer) | 1/4–1 | Small | Mob/escape | Nimble Escape — disengage or hide as bonus action |
| Hobgoblin (Warrior/Captain/Warlord) | 1/2–4 | Medium | Military formation | Martial Advantage — bonus damage when ally is adjacent to target |
| Bugbear (Stalker/Warrior) | 1–3 | Large | Solo ambush | Greatly increased damage on surprise attack |

Goblins rely on numbers and hit-and-run. Hobgoblins execute coordinated plans with clear ranks (Warlord has legendary actions). Bugbears are loners or small-gang predators; patience rewards their ambush style.

## Undead

Creatures animated by dark magic or trapped spiritual energy — reanimated corpses or spirits denied rest. Defined by shared immunities: poison damage, Poisoned condition, exhaustion; most are immune to Charmed and Frightened. No need to breathe, eat, drink, or sleep. Frequently deal or are empowered by necrotic damage; often vulnerable to radiant damage and sunlight.

| Undead | CR | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Skeleton | 1/4 | Animated bones; resistant to piercing from arrows |
| Zombie | 1/4 | Shambling corpse; Undead Fortitude can shrug off a killing blow |
| Ghoul | 1 | Flesh-devouring; claws paralyze humanoid victims on failed save |
| Specter | 1 | Incorporeal spirit; drains life, cannot carry physical objects |
| Wight | 3 | Dark-willed warrior; Life Drain reduces max HP, raises slain as zombie thralls |
| Ghost | 4 | Ethereal spirit tethered to location or purpose; can possess humanoids |
| Wraith | 5 | Powerful incorporeal; drains max HP, spawns Specters from those it kills |
| Vampire Spawn | 5 | Blood-drinking thrall bound to its vampire master |
| Vampire | 13 | Legendary apex: regenerates, charms, climbs walls, shapeshifts; weaknesses — sunlight, running water, wooden stake |
| Lich | 21 | Archmage who achieved undeath via phylactery; reconstitutes unless phylactery is destroyed |

**Scaling threat:** Low-CR undead (Skeletons, Zombies) dangerous in masses; mid-tier (Wights, Wraiths) punish parties lacking magical weapons through max-HP drain; high-CR (Vampires, Liches) are lair-based solo threats with legendary actions and regional effects. See [[iconic-monsters]] for deeper Lich and Vampire coverage.
