# Spellcasting Rules (2024)

Rules governing how spells are cast, limited, and resolved. These apply to all spellcasting classes unless a class feature specifies otherwise.

## Spell Levels

Every spell has a level from **0 to 9**. Level 0 spells are **cantrips** — simple, repeatable magic requiring no slot. Higher-level spells are more powerful and more taxing to cast.

## Spell Slots

Spell slots represent a spellcaster's daily magical reserves. Casting any level 1–9 spell expends one slot of that spell's level or higher. Slots of all levels are fully restored by completing a **Long Rest**.

- A level-3 Wizard has, for example, four level-1 slots and two level-2 slots.
- You can only expend one slot per turn (can't use the Magic action and also a Bonus Action slot on the same turn).

## Preparing Spells

Most classes maintain a **prepared list** of spells, updated at certain intervals:

| Class | When Preparation Changes | Spells Changed |
|-------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock | On gaining a level | One spell |
| Cleric, Druid, Wizard | After a Long Rest | Any number |
| Paladin, Ranger | After a Long Rest | One spell |

Some class features grant **always-prepared spells** that don't count against the prepared total.

## Cantrips

Cantrips (level 0) cost no spell slot and can be cast any number of times. They typically scale in power at character levels 5, 11, and 17.

## Ritual Casting

Spells tagged **Ritual** can be cast without expending a slot by adding 10 minutes to the casting time. The caster must have the spell prepared (the Wizard's Ritual Caster feature is an exception — they can ritual-cast from their spellbook without preparation).

## Casting without Slots

Ways to cast spells with no slot cost:
- Cantrips (inherently free)
- Ritual casting
- Class/racial special abilities with limited daily uses
- Magic items such as Spell Scrolls

## Upcasting (Higher-Level Slots)

Casting a spell in a slot of higher level than the spell's base level "upscales" it. Many spells gain improved effects — extra damage dice, more targets, longer duration — as noted in their descriptions.

## Components

Each spell requires one or more components, which must be provided or the spell fails:

| Component | Abbr. | Requirement |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| **Verbal** | V | Must speak words aloud in a normal voice; blocked by silence or being gagged |
| **Somatic** | S | Must perform gestures with at least one free hand |
| **Material** | M | Must have specific materials; a **Component Pouch** or **Spellcasting Focus** substitutes for non-costly, non-consumed materials |

Material components with a listed gold cost cannot be replaced by a component pouch or focus.

## Duration

- **Instantaneous** — the spell's effect occurs and ends immediately
- **Time Span** — lasts for a defined period (rounds, minutes, hours); can be dismissed early if the caster is not Incapacitated
- **Concentration** — see below

## Concentration

Certain ongoing spells require the caster to maintain **Concentration**:
- Only one Concentration spell can be active at a time; beginning a new one immediately ends the previous
- Taking damage forces a **Constitution saving throw** (DC = 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher) to maintain concentration
- Being Incapacitated or dying automatically breaks concentration
- Some features (e.g., the War Caster feat) grant Advantage on concentration saves

## Casting Time

Most spells use the **Magic action** (equivalent to the Attack action for spellcasters). Some use a **Bonus Action**, a **Reaction** (triggered by a specific event), or take 1 minute or more. Spells with casting times of 1 minute or longer require the Magic action on every turn and Concentration throughout; losing concentration cancels the spell without consuming a slot.

## Spell Save DC and Attack Modifier

Two key numbers govern a spellcaster's effectiveness:

> **Spell Save DC** = 8 + Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability modifier

> **Spell Attack Modifier** = Proficiency Bonus + Spellcasting Ability modifier

The spellcasting ability varies by class (Intelligence for Wizard, Wisdom for Cleric/Druid, Charisma for Sorcerer/Warlock/Bard, etc.).

## Range

- **Distance** — effect originates within a set number of feet from the caster
- **Touch** — caster must physically contact the target
- **Self** — affects only the caster or emanates from them

A clear path to the target is required (total cover blocks targeting).

## Combining Effects

Different spells' effects stack while their durations overlap. The same spell cast multiple times on the same target does not stack — only the most potent version applies.
