A Bastion is a player character's personal stronghold — home, refuge, and power base — developed over the course of a campaign. It is an optional system; the DM decides whether to include it, and individual players may opt in or out.

## Gaining a Bastion

Characters unlock a Bastion at **level 5**. Its form is up to the player: a wizard's tower, a cleric's shrine, a fighter's fortified keep, a rogue's guildhall. Multiple characters can merge their Bastions into one structure while keeping separate facility rosters.

## Bastion Turns

A **Bastion turn** represents a week (7 in-game days) of activity. Turns happen whether or not the character is present. On each Bastion turn the character issues orders; if absent and unable to communicate, the Bastion defaults to the *Maintain* order.

The DM can slow the cadence (e.g., one turn per month) if downtime periods are long, to prevent excessive benefit accumulation.

## Basic Facilities

Every Bastion starts with **two free basic facilities** (one Cramped, one Roomy). Options include: Bedroom, Dining Room, Parlor, Courtyard, Kitchen, Storage.

Basic facilities have no mechanical game effects but enrich roleplaying and setting. Additional basic facilities can be added or existing ones enlarged by spending gold and time.

| Space | Add Cost | Add Time | Enlarge Cost | Enlarge Time |
|-------|--------:|----------:|-------------:|-------------:|
| Cramped | 500 GP | 20 days | — | — |
| Roomy | 1,000 GP | 45 days | 500 GP | 25 days |
| Vast | 3,000 GP | 125 days | 2,000 GP | 80 days |

## Special Facilities

Special facilities are where game-mechanical benefits happen — crafting items, gathering resources, conducting research, recruiting allies. They are **gained through level advancement**, not purchased.

### Acquisition by Level

| Level | Total Special Facilities |
|:-----:|:------------------------:|
| 5 | 2 |
| 9 | 4 |
| 13 | 5 |
| 17 | 6 |

Each time a character gains a level they may swap one existing special facility for another they qualify for.

## Hirelings and Orders

Each special facility comes with **hirelings** — NPCs who staff and run it. The facility generates enough income to pay their wages. Hirelings are loyal and can be given personalities using NPC creation tools.

### Bastion Orders (issued each Bastion turn)

| Order | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Craft** | Hirelings produce an item the facility supports |
| **Empower** | The facility grants a temporary benefit to a creature |
| **Harvest** | Hirelings gather a resource produced in the facility |
| **Maintain** | All hirelings focus on upkeep; no other orders can be issued; triggers a Bastion Event roll |
| **Recruit** | Hirelings bring new creatures to the Bastion (including Defenders) |
| **Research** | Hirelings gather information |
| **Trade** | Hirelings buy or sell goods stored or produced in the facility |

## Bastion Defenders

Bastions can maintain a roster of **Defenders** — guards or fighters recruited via the Recruit order. Defenders are the first resource lost when the Bastion is attacked. The Bastion generates enough income to support them.

Defensive walls can be constructed (250 GP per 5-ft. square, 10 days per square); a fully enclosed Bastion reduces Defender losses during attacks.

## Bastion Events

Whenever the *Maintain* order is issued, the DM rolls on the **Bastion Events** table (d100):

| Roll | Event |
|:----:|-------|
| 01–50 | All Is Well (nothing significant) |
| 51–55 | Attack (Defenders lost; facility may shut down) |
| 56–58 | Criminal Hireling (pay bribe or lose hireling) |
| 59–63 | Extraordinary Opportunity (spend 500 GP for recognition + second roll) |
| 64–72 | Friendly Visitors (pay for facility use; earn 1d6 × 100 GP) |
| 73–76 | Guest (dignitary, refugee, mercenary, or friendly monster) |
| 77–79 | Lost Hirelings (one facility offline for a turn) |
| 80–83 | Magical Discovery (free Uncommon potion or scroll) |
| 84–91 | Refugees (2d4 displaced folk; earn 1d6 × 100 GP hospitality) |
| 92–98 | Request for Aid (send Defenders; risk loss but earn reward) |
| 99–00 | Treasure (art object or magic item) |

Events are resolved between turns and can be played as cutscenes with hirelings in focus.

## Fall of a Bastion

A Bastion can be lost through **divestiture** (voluntary abandonment), **neglect** (no orders issued for a number of turns equal to the character's level), or **ruination** (e.g., drawing the Ruin card from the Deck of Many Things). After a fall the character may establish a new Bastion with the same number of special facilities their level permits.
