A floating orb of malevolent flesh bristling with magical eyes, infamous as one of D&D's most iconic and paranoid predators.

**Role/type/CR:** Large aberration, CR 13. Roughly AC 18 (natural armor), ~180 HP. Has legendary actions and powerful lair actions when encountered in its lair.

**Signature abilities**
- *Antimagic Cone* — the large central eye constantly projects a cone of antimagic in whatever direction it faces, suppressing all spells and magical effects inside it.
- *Eye Rays (×10)* — each of the ten smaller eyestalks can unleash a different magical ray as a bonus action or legendary action: effects include charm, fear, slow, paralysis, petrification, telekinesis, sleep, enervation, disintegration, and a death ray.
- *Flight* — hovers continuously using innate levitation; does not need to touch the ground.
- *Legendary Resistance* — can choose to succeed on failed saving throws a limited number of times per day.

**Lore in brief**
Every beholder is unique in appearance and psychology, shaped by its own paranoid dreams — it is said that a sleeping beholder can accidentally dream a new aberration into existence. Their extreme xenophobia extends even to other beholders, whom they regard as inferior variants of themselves.
