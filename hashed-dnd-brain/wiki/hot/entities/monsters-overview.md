A factual map of how monsters are categorized in 5e (2024): the fourteen creature types, the Challenge Rating scale, and how a monster's power level is reflected in its stat block.

## The Fourteen Creature Types

Every monster belongs to exactly one creature type. Types have no mechanical rules of their own, but spells, class features, and magic items often interact with specific types. The fourteen types are:

| Type | Defining Characteristic |
|------|-------------------------|
| **Aberration** | Alien intellects with origins outside the known planes (e.g., aboleth, beholder, mind flayer) |
| **Beast** | Non-Humanoid natural animals; includes most giant animal variants (e.g., wolf, giant spider) |
| **Celestial** | Magical beings tied to the Upper Planes (e.g., angels, pegasus) |
| **Construct** | Magically crafted creatures, not born naturally (e.g., golem, homunculus, modron) |
| **Dragon** | Ancient scaly beings of great power; includes true dragons and dragon-kin (e.g., wyvern) |
| **Elemental** | Beings originating from the Inner/Elemental Planes (e.g., djinni, efreeti, water elemental) |
| **Fey** | Creatures bound to the Feywild or the forces of nature (e.g., dryad, pixie, goblin) |
| **Fiend** | Denizens of the Lower Planes (e.g., devil, demon, hell hound) |
| **Giant** | Towering humanoid-shaped beings, generally much larger than Humanoids (e.g., fire giant, troll, cyclops) |
| **Humanoid** | People defined by profession or role; can belong to any species (e.g., mage, knight, pirate) |
| **Monstrosity** | Unnatural creatures of strange or mixed origin (e.g., mimic, owlbear, manticore) |
| **Ooze** | Formless gelatinous creatures (e.g., black pudding, gelatinous cube) |
| **Plant** | Sentient or mobile vegetation and fungi (e.g., myconid, shambling mound, treant) |
| **Undead** | Reanimated corpses and lingering spirits (e.g., ghost, vampire, zombie, lich) |

A stat block may also carry **descriptive tags** in parentheses (e.g., "Fiend (Demon)"). These refine categorization for spells and abilities that reference subgroups, but carry no standalone rules.

## Challenge Rating

Challenge Rating (CR) is a single number that estimates how dangerous a monster is to a party of four characters. The scale runs from CR 0 (negligible threat) to CR 30 (most powerful creatures in the game). CR drives two concrete numbers in every stat block:

- **Experience Points (XP):** CR 1 = 200 XP; CR 10 = 5,900 XP; CR 20 = 25,000 XP; CR 30 = 155,000 XP.
- **Proficiency Bonus (PB):** Starts at +2 for CR 0–4, increases every four CR bands, reaching +9 at CR 29–30. PB feeds into the monster's saving throws, skill bonuses, and attack rolls.

CR is a planning tool, not a guarantee. Actual difficulty depends on terrain, action economy, and party resources. The *Dungeon Master's Guide* provides encounter-building rules that translate CR into a fuller difficulty estimate.

## How Monster Power Scales

Several stat block fields scale directly with a monster's CR:

- **Hit Points:** Larger size = bigger Hit Die (d4 for Tiny → d20 for Gargantuan); more dice = more HP.
- **Attack bonus and save DC:** Both benefit from the higher PB at higher CR.
- **Special abilities:** Higher-CR monsters tend to have more powerful recharging abilities, Legendary Actions, and lair-based powers.
