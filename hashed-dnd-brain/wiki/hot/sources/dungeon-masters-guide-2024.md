The *Dungeon Master's Guide (2024)* is the core reference for Dungeon Masters running 5th-edition D&D. While the *Player's Handbook* handles character-facing rules and the *Monster Manual* supplies stat blocks, this book focuses on everything the DM needs: running sessions, building encounters, designing adventures and campaigns, describing the planes, rewarding players with treasure, and optionally letting characters own strongholds.

## Chapter Outline

**Chapter 1 – The Basics**
Covers the DM's core roles (actor, director, improviser, referee, storyteller, teacher, worldbuilder), what a session looks like, how to structure play, and advice on communication, table safety, and play style.

**Chapter 2 – Running the Game**
Explains the three-step encounter loop (describe situation → players act → DM resolves), DC adjudication using the six-tier Typical DCs table, ability checks, group checks, advantage/disadvantage, saving throws, and social-interaction guidance. See [[running-the-game]].

**Chapter 3 – DM's Toolbox**
A reference chapter for situational rules: chases, alignment, doors, environmental effects, fear, hazards, marks of prestige, poisons, renown, settlements, traps, NPCs, and guidance for creating custom backgrounds, creatures, magic items, and spells. See [[dm-toolbox]].

**Chapter 4 – Creating Adventures**
Step-by-step adventure design, encounter objectives, pacing, and the revised combat encounter-building system (XP budget by party level and difficulty). Includes two sample adventures. See [[encounter-building]].

**Chapter 5 – Creating Campaigns**
Long-form campaign design: themes, arcs, factions, world-building, player hooks, and a complete sample campaign setting with three overarching conflicts.

**Chapter 6 – Cosmology**
A survey of the Great Wheel: the Material Plane, Feywild, Shadowfell, Astral Sea, Outer Planes, Inner Planes, and notable locations. Covers planar travel, portals, and how alignment shapes planar environments.

**Chapter 7 – Treasure**
Magic item categories (Common through Artifact), attunement, awarding treasure, random treasure tables keyed to CR, and an expanded magic item catalog including new items and revisions of older ones.

**Chapter 8 – Bastions**
Optional rules for characters at level 5+ to establish, build, and manage personal strongholds. Bastions grow over time and provide special facilities that offer in-game benefits between adventures.

**Appendices**
- A: Lore Glossary — iconic people and locations across the multiverse
- B: Maps — ready-to-use dungeon and settlement maps
- C: Tracking Sheets — downloadable DM reference sheets
