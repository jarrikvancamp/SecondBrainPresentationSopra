The *Monster Manual (2025)* is the Dungeon Master's reference for creature stat blocks in 5th edition D&D (2024 revision). It is a companion to the *Player's Handbook (2024)* and *Dungeon Master's Guide (2024)*, both of which are required to use this book.

## What the Book Contains

The MM is the primary source of game statistics for monsters — creatures the DM controls. It provides more than 500 stat blocks covering a wide range of threats, allies, and neutral creatures. Compared to the 2014 edition, every stat block was reviewed for balance and redesigned for legibility during play. Dozens of new monsters were added, including more high-CR threats. NPC stat blocks (Humanoids) now appear alongside other monsters and can represent any species, making them reusable across settings.

## How It Is Organized

| Section | Contents |
|---------|----------|
| How to Use a Monster | Overview of stat block structure and DM guidance |
| Monsters A–Z | Alphabetical entries; variants of the same monster are grouped together |
| Appendix A — Animals | Stat blocks for natural beasts, gathered separately |
| Appendix B — Monster Lists | Sorted lists by habitat, creature type, descriptive tag, group, and CR; also includes 2014→2025 stat-block conversion table |

Official D&D adventures reference monsters from this book. The *Dungeon Master's Guide* contains companion rules for encounter building and monster customization.

## How to Read a Stat Block

Each entry gives a monster's name, size, creature type (with optional descriptive tags), and alignment. The combat block covers AC, HP, Speed, and Initiative. Below that are the six ability scores with modifiers and saving throws, followed by optional fields (Skills, Resistances, Immunities, Gear, Senses, Languages). The lower sections list Traits, Actions, Bonus Actions, Reactions, and — for powerful monsters — Legendary Actions. Challenge Rating (CR) and the derived Proficiency Bonus (PB) appear in the stat block's details section.
