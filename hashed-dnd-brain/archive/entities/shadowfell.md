The Shadowfell — also called the Plane of Shadow — is a gloomy parallel dimension that occupies the same cosmological space as the [[material-plane|Material Plane]], acting as its dark and decayed reflection. See [[cosmology|Cosmology]] for the broader planar structure.

## Environment and Character

The Shadowfell shares the general geography of the Material Plane, but everything is twisted and ominous. Familiar landmarks become skull-shaped outcroppings, crumbling ruins, or sinister silhouettes. Forests hold grasping trees, and the sky above is a featureless black vault devoid of sun or stars.

A pervasive melancholy saturates the plane. Extended visits force Wisdom saving throws (DC 10); failure inflicts **Shadowfell Despair** — apathy, dread, or delusion that imposes Disadvantage on various rolls.

## Inhabitants

Shadow dragons, Undead of all types, cloakers, and darkmantles are among the Shadowfell's native denizens. The plane is intrinsically tied to death and necrotic energy.

## Shadow Crossings

**Shadow crossings** are thin spots in the veil between the Material Plane and the Shadowfell — dark corners of tombs, open graves, or haunted battlefields. They form in places steeped in death and shadow, and they seal shut the moment light touches them.

## Domains of Dread

Deep within the Shadowfell, mysterious entities called the **Dark Powers** maintain isolated demiplanes known as **Domains of Dread**. Wicked beings are drawn or trapped here as Darklords, imprisoned in realms that twist their darkest desires. Notable domains include:
- **Barovia** — the vampire Strahd von Zarovich's gothic prison-kingdom.
- **Mordent** — a realm haunted by restless dead.
- **Falkovnia** — overrun by an unending zombie plague.

The roiling Mists that surround each domain can slip across planes, dragging unwitting victims inside.

## Role in the Cosmology

Like the Feywild, the Shadowfell can be used as a path between worlds of the Material Plane, though it is rarely mapped and even more rarely safe.
