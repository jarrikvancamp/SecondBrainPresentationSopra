# Equipment (2024)

Chapter 6 covers gear, currency, weapons, armor, and tools. Sold equipment fetches half its purchase price; trade goods (gems, art objects) retain full value.

## Currency

Five coin denominations, all relative to the Gold Piece (GP):

| Coin | Abbr. | Value in GP |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Copper Piece  | CP | 1/100 |
| Silver Piece  | SP | 1/10  |
| Electrum Piece | EP | 1/2  |
| Gold Piece    | GP | 1     |
| Platinum Piece | PP | 10   |

Coins weigh about 1/3 oz each; 50 coins = 1 lb.

## Weapons

### Categories

- **Simple vs. Martial** — proficiency is typically granted by class (e.g., "Simple weapons" or "Martial weapons")
- **Melee vs. Ranged** — melee attacks targets within 5 ft; ranged uses normal/long range in feet (Disadvantage beyond normal range, can't attack beyond long range)

### Key Weapon Properties

| Property | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Ammunition** | Requires ammo to fire; half can be recovered after a fight |
| **Finesse** | Use Strength or Dexterity modifier (same modifier for both rolls) |
| **Heavy** | Disadvantage if melee and Str < 13, or ranged and Dex < 13 |
| **Light** | Enables dual-wielding bonus attack without adding ability modifier to damage |
| **Loading** | Only one shot per action/bonus action/reaction |
| **Reach** | Adds 5 ft to melee reach |
| **Thrown** | Can be thrown for a ranged attack; melee weapons use their normal ability modifier |
| **Two-Handed** | Requires both hands to attack |
| **Versatile** | Can be used one- or two-handed; listed damage applies two-handed |

### Weapon Mastery (2024 new feature)

Each weapon has one mastery property. You must have a feature (e.g., the Warrior class's Weapon Mastery) to use it. Properties and representative weapons:

| Mastery | Effect | Example Weapons |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| **Cleave** | On hit, free attack vs. adjacent second target (no ability mod added) | Greataxe, Halberd |
| **Graze** | On a miss, deal ability-modifier damage of the weapon's type | Glaive, Greatsword |
| **Nick** | Make dual-weapon extra attack as part of the Attack action (not Bonus Action) | Dagger, Scimitar |
| **Push** | On hit, push target up to 10 ft away (Large or smaller) | Greatclub, Pike |
| **Sap** | On hit, target has Disadvantage on its next attack before your next turn | Mace, Longsword |
| **Slow** | On hit, reduce target's Speed by 10 ft until your next turn | Club, Javelin |
| **Topple** | On hit, target makes Con save or gains Prone condition | Quarterstaff, Battleaxe |
| **Vex** | On hit, you have Advantage on your next attack vs. that creature | Handaxe, Rapier |

## Armor

### Categories and AC

| Category | Don Time | AC Calculation | Dex Cap | Strength Req. |
|----------|----------|----------------|---------|----------------|
| **Light** | 1 min | 11–12 + full Dex mod | None | None |
| **Medium** | 5 min | 12–15 + Dex mod | Max +2 | None |
| **Heavy** | 10 min | 14–18 (fixed) | None | Str 13 or 15 |
| **Shield** | Utilize action | +2 AC | — | None |

Some armor types impose Disadvantage on Stealth (Dexterity) checks.

### Representative Armor Costs

| Armor | AC | Cost |
|-------|----|------|
| Leather (light) | 11 + Dex | 10 GP |
| Studded Leather (light) | 12 + Dex | 45 GP |
| Chain Shirt (medium) | 13 + Dex (max 2) | 50 GP |
| Breastplate (medium) | 14 + Dex (max 2) | 400 GP |
| Chain Mail (heavy) | 16 | 75 GP |
| Plate Armor (heavy) | 18 | 1,500 GP |
| Shield | +2 | 10 GP |

### Armor Proficiency Requirement

Wearing armor without proficiency imposes Disadvantage on all Strength and Dexterity checks (including attack rolls and saving throws using those abilities) and prevents spellcasting. A character can only wear one suit of armor and wield one shield at a time.

## Tools

Tools let you make specialized ability checks or craft items. Each tool entry specifies:
- **Ability** — the ability score used with tool checks
- **Utilize** — actions you can take with the tool and their DCs
- **Craft** — items producible with the tool (using crafting rules)

Having tool proficiency adds your Proficiency Bonus to checks; if also proficient in a relevant skill, you gain Advantage.

### Tool Categories

- **Artisan's Tools** (16 types, e.g., Alchemist's Supplies 50 GP, Smith's Tools 20 GP, Tinker's Tools 50 GP) — focused on crafting trade goods
- **Other Tools** — Disguise Kit (25 GP), Forgery Kit (15 GP), Gaming Sets (varies), Herbalism Kit (5 GP), Musical Instruments (varies), Navigator's Tools (25 GP), Poisoner's Kit (50 GP), Thieves' Tools (25 GP)

## Adventuring Gear

A large variety of general-purpose items is listed in Chapter 6 (rope, torches, rations, backpacks, lanterns, healing kits, etc.). Each has a listed weight and cost.
