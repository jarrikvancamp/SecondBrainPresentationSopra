The D&D multiverse is an interconnected web of planes of existence — distinct dimensions, each shaped by elemental forces, alignment, or the interplay of mortal life and magic. Most adventures take place on the **Material Plane**, but the other planes exert constant influence on it.

## Structure: The Great Wheel

The dominant model organises the planes as concentric wheels. The Material Plane (with its echo-planes) sits at the hub; the Inner Planes ring it; the Outer Planes form the outermost wheel, arranged by alignment. The spatial arrangement is largely metaphorical — no observer can verify it directly.

Good-aligned Outer Planes are called **Upper Planes** (home to Celestials); evil-aligned ones are **Lower Planes** (home to Fiends). A creature visiting a plane with an opposing alignment experiences *planar dissonance*.

## Planar Travel

- **Portals** — Stationary links between specific locations. May require a key item, command word, or particular circumstances to open. Sigil, the City of Doors (atop the Outlands' Spire), hosts an extraordinary concentration of portals.
- **Spells** — *Plane Shift*, *Gate*, *Etherealness*, and *Astral Projection* all provide direct or indirect planar access.
- **Transitive routes** — Traversing the Ethereal Plane reaches the Inner Planes; the Astral Plane routes travel to the Outer Planes via colour pools.
- **Planar roads** — The Infinite Staircase, River Oceanus (Upper Planes), River Styx (Lower Planes), and Yggdrasil the World Tree connect multiple planes.
